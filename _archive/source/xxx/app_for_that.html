There really is an App for that


So here are a few apps that should get you going in the Netherlands. From translation, to public transport, and, rain forecasts.


Google basics

Google will be your friend here, for translation and telling you where you are.



Google Maps

Is at the moment the best way to find and calculate how to get from A to B. It includes public transport, biking and walking.It may want to switch your language to dutch, but if you are logged in, it's all in the settings.





Google Translate

The best way to translate a website, or find out what that ingredient is. Has a live translate function now, for conversations, which is a good last resort. But, a smile and english goes very far here.


Getting around


9292

The best way to plan a trip if you are using public transport. Includes busses, trams, and metro. A bit more reliable than Google Maps, also gives you an estimate cost.



OV Chipkaart

A very pink app about the balance of your chipcard, can also tell you when you checked on and off, and recharge points. Handy if you run out half way somewhere. They also post nearest top-up points on the bus, tram stop maps as well.



NS Reisplanner

Fully train orientated, so unless you are catching the train it might not be that useful. It includes the timetable, delays, your chipcard details and subscriptions. Which includes the 40% offpeak Korting.



Weather

Staying dry could be the case of leaving 5 minutes earlier, or 2 minutes later here. Rain can be brutal. So just short of controlling the weather, the dutch have a great deal of weather reporting.



Buienradar

Basically shows you where the rain, sleet or snow is at any point in time. There you have to make your own mind up.



Buienalarm

Shows you a graph of your area and the amount of rain, sleet or snow you are going to get in the next couple of hours. You can also set it up for notifications.


Food

You can use these apps, basically when you are hungry and lazy for ordering food online for delivery. No humans involved except for the delivery guy. Very useful when its cold outside, or you have no desire to walk to Albert Heijn.



Just Eat

Food ordering with delivery app.



Thuizbezorged

Food ordering with delivery app, Shannon and Hans say the online site actually accepts American cards, which is good to know.




Housing

A few Apps that are good when looking for a house or a room.



Kamernet

Room finding app, free to look, but you need to subscribe to apply for a room.Many new rooms on offer everyday, pretty legit, also online.



Funda

Real-estate app. Good if you are lookinh for a whole apartment.
